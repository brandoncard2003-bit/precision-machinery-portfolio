## Desktop Table Saw
**Type:** Machining
**Tools:** HAAS Manual Lathe, HAAS Manual Mill, HAAS CNC Mill
**Tolerance:** 0.0005 inches
**Material:** Aluminum, Cold-Rolled Steel

A 13-piece desktop table saw built from aluminum and cold-rolled steel. 
Required sourcing carbide milling tools and calculating precise RPM and feed 
rates for cutting steel. CNC G-Code was written and executed on a HAAS CNC 
mill for select components. Tolerances as tight as 0.0005 inches were 
achieved throughout the build.

## Aluminum Tape Dispenser
**Type:** Machining
**Tools:** HAAS CNC Mill, HAAS CNC Lathe
**Tolerance:** 0.005 inches
**Material:** Aluminum, Brass

A 7-piece tape dispenser machined from aluminum and brass. Walls were milled 
on a HAAS CNC mill and screws were turned on a HAAS CNC lathe. The project 
required use of the A-axis to rotate parts, including finding the correct 
zero home position. All parts manufactured to a tolerance of 0.005 inches.

## Drum Kit Boot
**Type:** 3D Printing | Original Design
**Tools:** Tinkercad, Creality Ender 3
**Material:** Ataraxia Art Black FPLA Filament

Designed and printed a replacement boot for a drum kit from scratch after 
finding no suitable off-the-shelf replacement. Created original blueprints 
and tolerances independently. Iterated through a failed first prototype 
by identifying and correcting measurement errors in the inside diameter, 
outside diameter, and length. Final print completed in 9 hours 51 minutes 
on a Creality Ender 3.

## Phoenix Christmas Ornament
**Type:** 3D Printing | Original Design
**Tools:** Autodesk Fusion, Creality Ender 3
**Material:** PLA Filament

Designed an original Phoenix ornament from concept sketches through to a 
finished 3D print. Went through three concept sketches before settling on 
a final design. Blueprint dimensions were derived by measuring reference 
images and refining in Fusion. Troubleshot a failed print caused by bed 
adhesion issues, resolving it with a Nano Polymer Adhesive and adjusting 
the hole diameter from 0.027 to 0.114 inches. Final print completed in 
1 hour 22 minutes at 15% infill using a triangle infill pattern.

> 📝 **Design decisions:** Three concepts were sketched before settling 
> on a final design. The first was discarded because the feathers would 
> be too complex to model in CAD. The second was discarded because the 
> tail feather dimensions could not be calculated. The third was chosen 
> because the wings and tail were achievable in Fusion.

## Guitar Body
**Type:** 3D Printing
**Tools:** Creality Ender 3
**Material:** Red/Black Multicolor PLA, Black Silk PLA Filament

A multi-piece 3D printed electric guitar body assembled from hexagonal 
panel pieces. Printed at 65% infill density to withstand string tension 
forces. Copper shielding was installed in the body cavities to prevent 
electromagnetic interference with the pickups and electronics.
