# Phoenix Christmas Ornament

[← Back to Portfolio](../README.md)

**Type:** 3D Printing | Original Design
**Tools:** Autodesk Fusion, Creality Ender 3
**Material:** PLA Filament

---

## Overview

Designed an original Phoenix ornament from concept sketches through to a 
finished 3D print. Went through three concept sketches before settling on 
a final design. Blueprint dimensions were derived by measuring reference 
images and refining in Fusion. Troubleshot a failed print caused by bed 
adhesion issues, resolving it with a Nano Polymer Adhesive and adjusting 
the hole diameter from 0.027 to 0.114 inches. Final print completed in 
1 hour 22 minutes at 15% infill using a triangle infill pattern.

---

## Design Decisions

Three concepts were sketched before settling on a final design. The first was discarded because the feathers would be too complex to model in CAD. The second was discarded because the tail feather dimensions could not be calculated. The third was chosen because the wings and tail were achievable in Fusion.

## Finished Piece

<img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a441365f-764d-499f-8872-9a507e4627fa" width="300"/>
<img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6046bdfd-44f8-406b-9f27-00ff377cf8ae" width="300"/>

> ✅ **What was fixed:** Re-leveled the print bed and applied Nano Polymer 
> Adhesive from Vision Miner to improve adhesion. Increased the hole 
> diameter from 0.027 to 0.114 inches. Fillets of 0.009 inches were added 
> around all edges for a smooth finish. Printed at 15% infill with a 
> triangle infill pattern in Creality Slicer 4.8.
